Growing Up in Brooklyn

I grew up in a vibrant mix of Caribbean and West African cultures. As a Guinean-American, my childhood was shaped by a blend of traditions — weekends spent attending Quranic school and dance classes, and helping my dad at his clothing store. Growing up around family entrepreneurs taught me responsibility, perseverance, and the value of hard work from an early age. These experiences instilled in me a strong work ethic and a love for creativity, which continue to shape my academic pursuits, professional goals, and personal passions today.

Lash Tech

My journey as a lash tech began during quarantine, when I would just practice on dolls out of boredom. Growing up surrounded by family entrepreneurs, I had always been drawn to creativity and business. Once I arrived at college, I started practicing on my roommate, and by the summer of 2024, I took a professional lash course. In July 2024, I officially launched my business, and ever since, I have loved owning and managing my own venture. Running my business has taught me discipline, customer service, creativity, and the joy of helping people feel confident and beautiful.

Skateboarding

When I was younger, my father bought me a penny board, and I absolutely loved it. Over the years, I grew out of it, but this summer I picked it back up. Since then, I’ve been going to Prospect Park in the mornings to ride my skateboard. It’s incredibly freeing and reminds me of the joy of rediscovering something I love.

Traveling

Traveling has been a passion of mine from a young age. I’ve visited countries like Guinea, Belgium, France, and London (as pictured below), and each trip gives me a sense of freedom and the chance to immerse myself in different cultures. In the future, I hope to travel even more and eventually live in another country. I’m especially excited to be going abroad to London next semester!

School & Social Life

I love my friends, and the community I’ve built here at Syracuse is truly amazing. I never expected to find people who understand me and push me to be better. I’ve found communities that reflect all parts of my identity, and I’m so grateful for that.
