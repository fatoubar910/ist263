Personal Page

Growing Up in Brooklyn

I grew up in a vibrant mix of Caribbean and West African cultures. As a Guinean-American, my childhood was shaped by a blend of traditions — weekends spent attending Quranic school and dance classes, and helping my dad at his clothing store. Growing up around family entrepreneurs taught me responsibility, perseverance, and the value of hard work from an early age.

Lash Tech

My journey as a lash tech began during quarantine, when I practiced on dolls out of boredom. By summer 2024, I completed a professional lash course and launched my business. Running it has taught me discipline, customer service, creativity, and the joy of helping people feel confident.

Skateboarding

When I was younger, my father bought me a penny board, and I absolutely loved it. Over the years, I grew out of it, but this summer I picked it back up. Since then, I’ve been going to Prospect Park in the mornings to ride my skateboard. It’s incredibly freeing.

Traveling

Traveling has been a passion of mine from a young age. I’ve visited countries like Guinea, Belgium, France, and London (as pictured below). Each trip gives me a sense of freedom and the chance to immerse myself in different cultures. I plan to travel more and eventually live abroad. I’m especially excited to go to London next semester!
