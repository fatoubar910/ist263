Planets Slideshow

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. Its orbit around the Sun takes 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all the planets in the Solar System. It is named after the Greek god Hermes (Ερμής), translated into Latin Mercurius Mercury, god of commerce, messenger of the gods, mediator between gods and mortals.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. Venus orbits the Sun every 224.7 Earth days.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life.

Mars

Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere and surface features similar to the Moon.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, named after the Greek god of the sky.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun.
